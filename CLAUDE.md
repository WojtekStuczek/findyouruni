## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary**: High school students (17-19) exploring where to study in Europe. First major life decision — they need confidence, not overwhelm.
- **Secondary**: International students from outside Europe looking for English-taught programmes. Practical, comparison-driven.
- **Tertiary**: Parents helping their kids navigate options. They want credibility and clarity.

### Brand Personality
**Aspirational, guiding, trustworthy**

The interface should evoke: "I am doing something meaningful for my future. I am taking the next step." It should feel like a launchpad, not a spreadsheet. Beautiful and welcoming enough that a high schooler feels excited browsing it, organized enough that a parent trusts the data.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light — browsed during daytime research sessions, often on laptops/phones at home or school
- **Primary color**: Blue (`#2563eb`) — established brand, keep as anchor
- **Tone**: Clean, forward-looking, subtly aspirational. Not playful/gamified, not corporate/dry. The sweet spot between a polished SaaS tool and an inspiring editorial experience.
- **Anti-references**: Generic academic ranking tables (QS, THE), overly corporate portals, cluttered comparison sites

### Design Principles
1. **Progress, not pressure** — Every interaction should feel like a step forward, not a test. Guide, don't overwhelm.
2. **Data with soul** — Rankings and stats matter, but the experience should feel human and inviting, not clinical.
3. **Trust through craft** — High visual quality signals credibility. Parents and students trust a well-made tool.
4. **Mobile-native thinking** — Many users browse on phones during commutes or between classes. Performance and touch experience are non-negotiable.
5. **Room to grow** — The platform will evolve (affiliates, more features). Design decisions should leave space for that.
